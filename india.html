Native Trees of India

India's diverse climate supports a rich variety of native trees, many of which hold deep cultural and religious significance.

The Banyan Tree (Ficus benghalensis)

The national tree of India, the Banyan is famous for its massive canopy and its "prop roots" that grow down to form new trunks. A single Banyan can resemble a small forest and is considered sacred in Hinduism.

The Peepal Tree (Ficus religiosa)

Also known as the Bodhi tree, it is believed that Gautama Buddha attained enlightenment while meditating under a Peepal tree. It is recognized by its heart-shaped leaves with a long, extended tip.

The Neem Tree (Azadirachta indica)

Known as the "village pharmacy," nearly every part of the Neem tree has medicinal properties. It is widely used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for its antibacterial and antifungal qualities.
