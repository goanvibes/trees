Major Types of Trees

While there are thousands of species, most trees can be broadly categorized based on their foliage: Deciduous trees that lose their leaves, and Evergreen trees that keep them year-round.

Deciduous Trees

Deciduous trees shed their leaves annually, usually in the autumn. This is a survival strategy to conserve water and energy during cold or dry seasons. Their broad leaves are excellent at capturing sunlight.

  • Examples: Oak, Maple, Birch, Aspen
  • Characteristics: Broad leaves, seasonal leaf-fall, often produce flowers and fruits.

Evergreen Trees

Evergreen trees retain their leaves (or needles) year-round, shedding them gradually. This allows them to photosynthesize whenever conditions are favorable. Many evergreens are conifers, which reproduce using cones.

  • Examples: Pine, Spruce, Fir, Cedar
  • Characteristics: Typically have needles or scales, produce cones, and stay green all year.
