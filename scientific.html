The Science of Trees

A tree is a perennial plant with an elongated stem, or trunk, supporting branches and leaves. They are defined by their woody tissue and their ability to grow year after year.

Basic Anatomy

  • The Crown: The top part, including leaves and branches, where photosynthesis occurs.
  • The Trunk: Provides support and houses the vascular system to transport water and nutrients.
  • The Roots: Anchor the tree and absorb water and minerals from the soil.

Tree Rings

A tree's age can be found by counting its annual growth rings. The thickness of these rings reveals the environmental conditions of each year.
