Tree Species and Families

With over 60,000 different species, trees are classified into families based on their genetic and physical traits. Click on a family name below to learn more.

Fagaceae (The Beech Family)

Known for producing nuts like acorns and beechnuts. This family includes mighty trees like the Oak, Beech, and Chestnut. They are valued for their strong hardwood.

Pinaceae (The Pine Family)

These are conifers, recognizable by their needles and cones. Most are evergreen. This family includes Pine, Fir, Spruce, and Larch trees.

Rosaceae (The Rose Family)

Famous for beautiful flowers and delicious fruits. Many fruit trees belong to this family, including Apple, Cherry, Plum, and Pear.

Salicaceae (The Willow Family)

Often found near water, these trees, like the Willow, Poplar, and Aspen, are known for being fast-growing.
