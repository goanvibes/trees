Common Trees of Goa

Goa's coastal, tropical climate is a haven for a variety of lush, fruit-bearing trees that are deeply integrated into the local culture and cuisine.

Coconut Palm (Cocos nucifera)

The iconic tree of Goa, the Coconut Palm is often called the "tree of life" as every part of it is used â€“ from the fruit in Goan cuisine to the leaves for thatching roofs.

Mango Tree (Mangifera indica)

Goa is renowned for its many varieties of mangoes, with the Mancurado being one of the most prized. Mango trees are a common sight in gardens across the state.

Cashew Tree (Anacardium occidentale)

Introduced by the Portuguese, the cashew tree is famous for two products: the cashew nut and the cashew apple, which is used to produce Feni, Goa's traditional local spirit.
